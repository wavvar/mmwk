## 1. Product Overview

The Wavvar `F9A1` safety protection radar is a `77 GHz` millimeter-wave BSD blind-spot detection sensor designed for the rear of electric two-wheel and three-wheel vehicles. The module size is `44 × 60 × 13 mm`, the operating voltage is `12V DC`, and both factory-installed and aftermarket installation solutions are supported.

The product supports `BSD` blind-spot monitoring, `RCTA` rear cross traffic alert, `LCA` lane change assist, and `RCW` rear collision warning. It is rated `IP67` and is designed to operate in outdoor vibration, night-time, and humid environments.

## 3. Functional Definition and Operating Logic

### 3.1 BSD Blind Spot Detection

The millimeter-wave radar continuously scans the blind-spot area at the side-rear of the vehicle and detects surrounding moving targets. When a vehicle or obstacle enters the radar blind zone, the vehicle speed exceeds the trigger threshold (`20-30 km/h`, adjustable), and the relative distance is within `10 m`, the system keeps the corresponding warning lamp illuminated.

For front-installed systems, the signal can drive an instrument-cluster HMI indicator. For rear-installed systems, both warning lamps can remain on at low brightness as a caution signal. If the rider activates the turn signal to change lanes while the target is still in the blind zone, the system can trigger a buzzer alert or high-brightness flashing to indicate that lane changing is unsafe.

### 3.2 LCA Lane Change Assist

The system continuously monitors the adjacent lanes on both sides of the vehicle, covering the rear to side-rear region. When a fast-approaching vehicle is detected in the adjacent lane and a speed difference exists relative to the host vehicle, the system issues a warning.

If the target vehicle is within the `TTC` threshold, for example `TTC < 2.5 s`, the corresponding warning lamp remains at low brightness when the turn signal is off. If the rider turns on the turn signal, the corresponding side warning lamp flashes at high brightness and an audible reminder can also be issued.

### 3.3 RCTA Rear Cross Traffic Alert

This optional function is intended for electric vehicles equipped with a reverse gear. When the vehicle is shifted into reverse and begins to back up, the radar expands its scan to both sides behind the vehicle. If a pedestrian, vehicle, or other obstacle crosses the path behind the vehicle, the system immediately issues a warning.

The trigger condition is that a target enters the monitored area during reversing, within `10 m`, and the relative lateral speed or angular change falls within the `TTC` threshold, for example `TTC < 2.5 s`. Output methods include flashing the left and right mirror warning lamps and/or buzzer alarms.

### 3.4 RCW Rear Collision Warning

When the vehicle is moving straight or stationary, the system continuously monitors traffic directly behind it. If the radar detects a vehicle approaching from the rear at a dangerous closing speed, the system issues a warning.

The trigger condition is that the relative speed difference exceeds a threshold and the distance drops below a safe value, or the target enters the `TTC` threshold, for example `TTC < 2.5 s`. When `RCW` is triggered, the system can flash both rear-view mirror warning lamps and generate a buzzer reminder for both the approaching vehicle and the host rider.

## 4. Interfaces and Installation

### 4.1 Power and Output Harness

The device provides a `6-wire` harness, including:

- 2 signal output wires
- 2 lamp-control wires for the left and right warning lamps
- 2 power wires

### 4.2 Signal Output

The signal wires are connected to the vehicle controller or instrument-cluster `HMI` interface to trigger visual, acoustic, or combined warnings. The lamp-control wires directly drive the LED warning lamps on the left and right mirrors for independent side indication.

### 4.3 Installation Position

The radar should be fixed to the rear rack of the vehicle, with the radar face oriented horizontally toward the rear and kept free from obstruction such as cargo boxes or canopies.

For installation:

- `F9A1` requires a rear mounting plate and screws to secure the radar to the rear rack.
- `F9A1-D` is fixed directly to the rear rack using the matching screw set and applies only to specific compatible models.

### 4.4 Calibration and Debugging

After initial installation, the product should complete self-check and calibration. The `Wadar` app can be used over Bluetooth or Wi-Fi to view raw detection data and adjust parameters.
